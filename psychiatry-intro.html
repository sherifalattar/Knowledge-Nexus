Foreword · Opening statement
Empirical rigor, human fragility, and medical stewardship
Signed introduction

Behind every code in the pages that follow is a person in distress, and behind every weight is a promise: that a society has decided, in advance, what it will commit to that person’s care. To work at the seam between these two facts is to work where medicine becomes policy — and where policy, if it is careless, can quietly forget the medicine.

What this reference offers is small and exact. It is a single navigable correspondence among three systems that ordinarily do not meet: the clinical taxonomy of the DSM-5-TR, the coding of ICD-10-CM, and the grouping and relative weighting of the IR-DRG as the Department of Health of Abu Dhabi has adopted it through the Solventum classification. Each of these rests on decades of collective labor; none was built to be read against the others. The contribution is not to have replaced them, but to have placed them in conversation — with every figure verified against its primary source, and the provenance of each carried in the open.

It does not open the grouper, whose logic remains proprietary, nor does it pretend to dissolve the real tension between a clinical vocabulary that has moved on and an administrative one that has not. It makes that boundary legible. But legibility, here, is not a small thing: to see clearly how the language of suffering is rendered into the language of resource is the first condition of doing both faithfully — of paying for care in a way that remembers what care is for.

Dr. Sherif Alattar
