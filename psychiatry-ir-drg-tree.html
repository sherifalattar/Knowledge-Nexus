Bipolar Disorders
Relative Weight
SOI 1Minor2.4299
SOI 2Moderate / CC4.7934
SOI 3Major / MCC6.6878
Cardinal Features
  • Manic episode: elevated, expansive, or irritable mood with increased energy
  • Grandiosity or inflated self-esteem
  • Decreased need for sleep
  • Pressured speech or racing thoughts
  • Distractibility, impulsivity, impaired judgment, or high-risk behavior
  • Depressive episodes are common but not required for Bipolar I diagnosis
Resource Drivers
  • High safety risk
  • Agitation or behavioral escalation
  • Restraints / seclusion when clinically justified
  • Constant observation
  • Medication titration and stabilization
  • Longer stabilization with severe mania, psychosis, suicidality, or comorbidity

Schizophrenia Disorders
F20 / psychosisdiagnostic scope
Relative Weight
SOI 1Minor1.7420
SOI 2Moderate / CC3.4364
SOI 3Major / MCC4.6857
Cardinal Features
  • At least two DSM-5-TR Criterion A symptoms
  • At least one: delusions, hallucinations, or disorganized speech
  • Symptoms for a significant portion of at least 1 month, or less if treated
  • Continuous signs of disturbance for at least 6 months
  • Functional decline in work, relationships, or self-care
  • Negative symptoms may include avolition, flat affect, alogia, asociality, or anhedonia
Resource Drivers
  • Acute psychosis
  • Behavioral risk
  • Security / safety needs
  • High medication load
  • Prolonged stabilization

19418legacy label
Relative Weight
SOI 1Minor1.1223
SOI 2Moderate / CC2.2138
SOI 3Major / MCC3.3680
Cardinal Features
  • Persistent disturbance in eating or eating-related behavior
  • Restriction, avoidance, binge eating, purging, or compensatory behaviors
  • Body image disturbance or fear of weight gain when applicable
  • Malnutrition, weight instability, or medical compromise in severe restrictive illness

Resource Drivers

  • Medical-psychiatric stabilization and observation

Other Depressive Disorders
Relative Weight
SOI 1Minor1.3341
SOI 2Moderate / CC2.6317
SOI 3Major / MCC3.9588
Cardinal Features
  • Persistent depressive disorder / dysthymia: chronic depressed mood
  • Duration at least 2 years in adults or 1 year in children/adolescents
  • Never without Criteria A/B symptoms for more than 2 months
  • At least two: appetite, sleep, energy, self-esteem, concentration, or hopelessness
  • Other specified or unspecified depressive disorders causing distress or impairment
  • PMDD only when supported by the licensed IR-DRG diagnosis table
Resource Drivers
  • Lower baseline intensity
  • Escalates with complexity
  • Functional impact
  • Longer treatment duration than acute depression
  • Superimposed major depressive episodes when present

Key Takeaway

Base diagnosis sets the pathway — but clinical complexity, safety risk, comorbidities and severity (SOI) determine the final weight and reimbursement.

For authorizations, the 5-digit base DRG is generally used pre-admission. At discharge, the 6th digit reflects Severity of Illness based on final documentation and grouper logic. The 7th digit reflects Risk of Mortality where supported by grouper logic, but DOH Abu Dhabi payment calculation is driven by the applicable DOH relative-weight rules.
Source: Department of Health - Abu Dhabi, IR-DRG Relative Weight Addendum, Appendix A.
