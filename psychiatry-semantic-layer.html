01 Legal & licensing

Licensed IR-DRG logic is proprietary

IR-DRG — Solventum (formerly 3M Health Information Systems)

International Refined Diagnosis Related Groups (IR-DRG) is a licensed, proprietary classification system owned by Solventum (formerly 3M Health Information Systems), historically derived from the AP-DRG logic family.

The grouper algorithm, diagnosis tables, MDC assignments, CC/MCC logic, PCAT tables, severity splits, DRG weights, and DRG definitions are licensed intellectual property and are not reproduced here. No legal open-source IR-DRG grouper exists.

The diagnosis → MDC → base-group architecture shown in the map is a navigational and educational layer built on public ICD-10-CM (CDC/NCHS) and CMS MS-DRG Appendix B mappings. It is DRG-inspired; it is not the licensed IR-DRG table and must not be represented as IR-DRG.

“This model is DRG-inspired but not IR-DRG. It uses only publicly available ICD-10-CM and CMS MS-DRG data. Every operational diagnosis-to-MDC and base-group assignment must be verified against the licensed IR-DRG DX table or grouper.” — “IR-DRG” and the Solventum name are trademarks of Solventum, used here for reference and attribution only.

04 Provenance constraints

Three lanes, one validation rule

Public lane

ICD-10-CM + CMS MS-DRG

Open and citable. Supplies the block structure, the labels, and the navigational MDC proxy. Used for orientation, never as the payer's grouping authority.

Licensed lane

IR-DRG DX table / grouper

Solventum proprietary. The authority for operational diagnosis → MDC → base group → severity. Not reproduced; consulted via the licensed grouper or DRG browser.

DOH-published lane

Addendum 05 · Appendix A

The relative weights, ALOS, and trim boundaries — public because DOH published them in the V2025.1 rules. The only IR-DRG-adjacent figures that are openly citable.

Validation rule. No diagnosis is assigned to an MDC or base group in adjudication on the strength of this layer alone — each is confirmed against the licensed DX table. Every ICD-10-CM code printed in this reference has been checked against the ICD-10-CM tabular (FY2026).

04A Coding governance notes

Abu Dhabi coding frame and exclusions

Diagnosis coding

ICD-10-CM, not

Abu Dhabi claims use ICD-10-CM diagnosis guidance. references are removed from this file because is the Australian modification and is not the Abu Dhabi diagnosis-coding standard.

Procedure coding

CPT-4 / HCPCS

Level I HCPCS is CPT, maintained by the AMA. Level II HCPCS uses one letter followed by four numbers and is used for additional reporting codes where assigned in Abu Dhabi.

Claims frame

DOH V2025.1 and addenda

The operational frame is the DOH claims and adjudication rules, ICD-10-CM diagnosis guidance, CPT-4/HCPCS procedure reporting, and the Solventum/3M IR-DRG grouper version tailored for Abu Dhabi.

Uncertain diagnosis and integral symptoms

Uncertain diagnosis at discharge: when documented as probable, suspected, likely, questionable, possible, to be ruled out, compatible with, consistent with, or similar terms of uncertainty, code the condition as if established where the applicable DOH coding manual instructs.

Integral symptoms - ICD-10-CM Official Guidelines I.B.7: signs and symptoms routinely associated with a confirmed diagnosis are not coded separately unless the ICD-10-CM classification instructs otherwise. Avoid separate coding for expected manifestations that do not require additional management.

06 Term lexicon

Definitions beneath the labels

Impulse-control disorders · F63

⚠ Grouping to verify. Base group 19412 is named “Personality & Impulse Control”, so F60–F63 maps there by label — but your UP-02 source lists F63 in the 19419 residual. Confirm against the licensed IR-DRG DX table before adjudication.
F63.1

Pyromania

19412 · verify

ICD-10-CMDeliberate, purposeful fire-setting on more than one occasion; tension or affective arousal before the act; fascination with fire and its situational contexts; pleasure or relief when setting fires or witnessing them.

DSM-5-TR / APANot done for monetary gain, ideology, to conceal crime, to express anger, or in response to delusion/impaired judgement. DSM-5-TR: impulse-control disorder.

F63.2

Kleptomania

19412 · verify

ICD-10-CMRecurrent failure to resist impulses to steal objects not needed for personal use or for their monetary value; rising tension before the theft, pleasure or relief during it.

DSM-5-TR / APANot committed to express anger or vengeance, and not in response to a delusion or hallucination. DSM-5-TR: impulse-control disorder.

F63.3

Trichotillomania

19412 · verify

ICD-10-CMRecurrent pulling out of one's own hair resulting in hair loss, with repeated attempts to decrease or stop, causing clinically significant distress or impairment.

DSM-5-TR / APADSM-5 reclassifies it under Obsessive-Compulsive & Related Disorders — ICD-10-CM still codes it F63.3 in the impulse block.

F63.81

Intermittent explosive disorder

19412 · verify

ICD-10-CMRecurrent behavioural outbursts reflecting a failure to control aggressive impulses — verbal or physical aggression grossly out of proportion to any provocation or precipitating stressor.

DSM-5-TR / APAOutbursts are impulsive (not premeditated) and not committed for tangible gain. DSM-5-TR: Disruptive, Impulse-Control & Conduct Disorders.

F63.0

Pathological gambling

19412 · verify

ICD-10-CMPersistent, recurrent maladaptive gambling that disrupts personal, family, or occupational functioning.

DSM-5-TR / APADSM-5 reclassifies it as Gambling Disorder under Substance-Related & Addictive Disorders — the only behavioural addiction in that chapter.

Gender, sexual & psychosexual · parked · reference only

F64.0

Transsexualism

parked

ICD-10-CMPersistent desire to live and be accepted as a member of the opposite sex, with discomfort about one's anatomical sex and often a wish for hormonal/surgical alignment.

DSM-5-TR / APADSM-5-TR replaces it with Gender Dysphoria — the diagnosis rests on clinically significant distress about incongruence, not on identity itself. ICD-11 moves it out of mental disorders (Gender Incongruence).

F64.1

Dual-role transvestism

parked

ICD-10-CMCross-dressing to experience temporary membership in the opposite gender role, without sexual motivation and without desire for permanent transition.

DSM-5-TR / APADSM-5-TR keeps no equivalent standalone diagnosis; cross-gender expression without distress is not a disorder.

F65.1

Transvestic fetishism

parked

ICD-10-CMCross-dressing primarily for sexual arousal.

DSM-5-TR / APADSM-5-TR: Transvestic Disorder only where it causes distress/impairment or involves non-consenting others.

F65

Paraphilic disorders

parked

ICD-10-CMFetishism, exhibitionism, voyeurism, and related atypical-interest patterns. ICD-10-CM F65 = Paraphilias.

DSM-5-TR / APADSM-5-TR distinguishes a paraphilia (atypical interest alone) from a paraphilic disorder (interest plus distress/impairment or harm/non-consent).

F52

Sexual dysfunctions

parked

ICD-10-CMPsychogenic dysfunctions of desire, arousal, orgasm, or sexual pain not due to a substance or known condition.

DSM-5-TR / APADSM-5-TR reorganizes these into more granular, gender-specific categories (e.g. Genito-Pelvic Pain/Penetration Disorder).

F66

Other sexual disorders

parked

ICD-10-CMICD-10-CM F66 = “Other sexual disorders” — a single code with no subdivisions.

DSM-5-TR / APALabel correction: the WHO title “…associated with sexual development and orientation” and codes F66.0/F66.1 do not exist in ICD-10-CM.

Terminology bridges

F40–F48

“Neuroses”

terminology

ICD-10-CMA legacy DRG umbrella label covering anxiety, phobic, obsessive-compulsive, stress-related, dissociative, and somatoform disorders.

DSM-5-TR / APADSM-5-TR does not use “neurosis” entirely, using Anxiety, Obsessive-Compulsive & Related, Trauma- & Stressor-Related, Somatic Symptom, and Dissociative Disorders.

F10–F19

Substance use

terminology

ICD-10-CMICD-10-CM separates use, abuse, and dependence into distinct sub-codes per substance.

DSM-5-TR / APADSM-5 collapses abuse/dependence into one Substance Use Disorder graded by severity (mild / moderate / severe).

07 Corrected clinical manifestations

Three distinctions the labels hide

Diagnostic safeguards — apply before any category is used as a DRG-navigation proxy

For every psychiatric manifestation below, confirm duration, clinically significant functional impairment, exclusion of substance / medication effects, exclusion of another medical condition, cultural and contextual appropriateness, and the relevant mood-disorder and psychotic-disorder differentials before assigning a clinical category — and never assign an MDC or base group on the strength of a manifestation summary alone.

A manifestation list describes what a disorder can look like. It is not the diagnostic threshold, the ICD-10-CM coding instruction, or the licensed IR-DRG grouping rule — those are three separate layers, each verified on its own. This page surfaces the clinical layer; coding and grouping stay with their authorities.
19410 · F20

Schizophrenia is not all psychosis

corrected

Schizophrenia is one specific disorder within the broader DSM-5-TR chapter Schizophrenia Spectrum and Other Psychotic Disorders. Other psychotic disorders, including brief psychotic disorder, schizophreniform disorder, schizoaffective disorder, delusional disorder, substance/medication-induced psychotic disorder, and psychotic disorder due to another medical condition, should not automatically be assumed to group to 19410 without licensed IR-DRG diagnosis-table verification.

DSM-5-TR requires the Criterion A symptom set: at least two characteristic domains for a significant portion of a 1-month period, or less if successfully treated. At least one must be delusions, hallucinations, or disorganized speech.

A1 DelusionsA2 HallucinationsA3 Disorganized speech A4 Grossly disorganized or catatonic behaviorA5 Negative symptoms

These sit alongside Criterion B: marked functional decline in work, interpersonal relations, or self-care below the prior level, and the total duration requirement of at least six months.

Differential exclusions — rule out before locking F20. Because psychosis is a feature of many conditions, schizophrenia is a diagnosis of positive criteria plus exclusion, not the default landing point for any psychotic presentation:

RULE OUT Schizoaffective disorder — mood episodes concurrent with psychosis RULE OUT Major depressive / bipolar disorder with psychotic features RULE OUT Substance- / medication-induced psychotic disorder RULE OUT Psychotic disorder due to another medical condition RULE OUT Delirium — acute, fluctuating, attention-impaired RULE OUT Culturally sanctioned belief or experience
19411

Major Depression is episodic and higher-threshold

corrected

Major depressive disorder is distinguished by an episodic depressive syndrome with a higher symptom threshold. It requires at least 5 symptoms during the same 2-week period, and at least one must be depressed mood or markedly diminished interest or pleasure.

19414 · F34.1

Other Depressive Disorders emphasize chronicity or subthreshold presentation

corrected

Persistent depressive disorder / dysthymia is chronic low-to-moderate depressive illness lasting at least 2 years in adults, or 1 year in children and adolescents. Depressed mood is present for most of the day, for more days than not, and the individual is never without Criteria A and B symptoms for more than 2 months at a time.

Poor appetite or overeatingInsomnia or hypersomniaLow energy / fatigue Low self-esteemPoor concentration / indecisionFeelings of hopelessness

Other specified or unspecified depressive disorders capture clinically significant depressive presentations that do not meet full criteria for major depressive disorder or persistent depressive disorder. PMDD should be included only when supported by the licensed IR-DRG diagnosis table.

07A Boundary criteria & spectrum splits

Where an elegant summary needs a sharper edge

The cards above correct three specific confusions. These add the diagnostic boundaries an adjudication-grade reference needs — the episode distinctions, severity anchors, and umbrella categories that a single label quietly compresses.

19413 · F31

Bipolar separates into mania, hypomania, and bipolar depression

expanded

A bipolar label is not one clinical state. The pole and its severity drive presentation, monitoring need, and admission logic, so the three episode types must be distinguished rather than merged into “elevated mood”:

MANIA Abnormally elevated, expansive, or irritable mood with increased activity/energy, ≥1 week (or any duration if hospitalised), causing marked impairment, hospitalisation, or psychosis HYPOMANIA Same symptom picture, ≥4 days, observable change in functioning but without marked impairment, hospitalisation, or psychosis BIPOLAR DEPRESSION A major depressive episode occurring in a patient with a bipolar diagnosis — the depressive pole, not unipolar MDD

Bipolar I requires at least one manic episode; bipolar II requires at least one hypomanic episode and at least one major depressive episode, with no history of mania. For recognition, assessment, and treatment of mania, hypomania, and bipolar depression, defer to the current NICE bipolar guidance as the clinical anchor; grouping remains with the licensed IR-DRG table.

19416 · F50

“Eating disorders” is an umbrella — the criterion layer is per-disorder

expanded

As an umbrella the section is clinically sound, but each disorder carries its own anchor. The presence or absence of body-image disturbance and of compensatory behaviour is what separates them — and what a reviewer must see documented:

Anorexia nervosa Energy restriction → significantly low weight, intense fear of weight gain, body-image disturbance Bulimia nervosa Recurrent binge eating plus recurrent compensatory behaviour; weight often normal Binge-eating disorder Recurrent binge eating without regular compensatory behaviour ARFID Avoidance/restriction without body-image disturbance or fear of weight gain OSFED / UFED Clinically significant but not meeting full criteria for the above

Medical compromise (electrolyte derangement, bradycardia, hypotension, refeeding risk) is what moves several of these from ambulatory to inpatient logic — a severity anchor, not a diagnostic one. Confirm the specific disorder against the licensed IR-DRG diagnosis table before grouping; defer to current NICE eating-disorders guidance for identification, monitoring, and care-setting.

multiple

Other compressed umbrellas that must be unpacked before adjudication

note

Two further groupings read cleanly as labels but hide heterogeneous diagnoses with different thresholds and grouping behaviour. They are flagged here so a summary is never adjudicated as if it were a single diagnosis:

Childhood-onset disorders Neurodevelopmental (ASD, ADHD, intellectual disability, communication, specific learning, tic) vs disruptive/conduct vs emotional disorders of childhood — distinct chapters, distinct criteria Personality & impulse-control Personality disorders (enduring, pervasive, trait-based) are a different construct from the F63 impulse-control disorders detailed in the lexicon above — do not merge

For each, the operative grouping must be confirmed against the licensed IR-DRG DX table; the manifestation summary orients, it does not adjudicate.

DSM-5-TR currency — read precisely. The clinical overlay is the DSM-5-TR base edition, with APA-posted approved updates through September 2025 where applicable. The September 2025 supplement is a targeted set of disorder- and code-level changes (for example, the expansion to Moral, Religious, or Spiritual Problem at Z65.8, cyclothymic-disorder wording, and provider-assessment phrasing in the substance-use criteria) — it is not a global rewrite of psychiatric criteria. Any criteria change must be checked at disorder level against the APA-posted update before a diagnostic or coding revision is made. For recognition and assessment of depression, bipolar disorder, and the eating disorders, anchor to the current published NICE guidance for each.
